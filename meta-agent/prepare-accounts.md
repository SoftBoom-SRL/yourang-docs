# Prepare your Meta accounts

Everything you need to sort out on Facebook and Instagram before connecting the agent, explained one click at a time.

_Before connecting the agent you need to prepare your Facebook and Instagram accounts. These are one-time steps you carry out from your phone or computer and take just a few minutes. Every single step is explained here, with nothing taken for granted: if you follow the instructions in order, you cannot go wrong._

> [!TIP]
> **You don't need to be technical**
> You don't need to install anything, write any code, or create accounts for «developers». You only need the Instagram app, the Facebook app or website, and the free Meta Business Suite tool. If you can use Instagram and Facebook, you can do all of this.

## The checklist: what you need to have at the end <a id="cosa-prepari"></a>

By the end of this page you will need all four of the following things ready. We go through them one at a time in the sections below; this is just the checklist so you know where you're headed.

1. **A Facebook Page you administer** — A «Page» is your business's public profile on Facebook, separate from your personal profile. You must be an administrator of it, meaning you have full control. If you don't have one, further down I'll explain how to create it.
2. **A «professional» Instagram profile** — It must be of the Business or Creator type, not personal. It's free and takes one minute to switch from the Instagram app.
3. **Instagram linked to the Facebook Page** — The two accounts must be associated with each other: this link is what allows Instagram messages to be managed through the Page.
4. **The «Allow access to messages» toggle enabled on Instagram** — A setting inside Instagram that authorizes connected tools to read and manage your direct messages. If it's off, the agent will not see Instagram DMs.

## Step 0 — Don't have a Facebook Page yet? Create one <a id="non-hai-pagina"></a>

If you already have a Facebook Page for your business, skip this step and go to Step 1. If you don't have one, create it now: you need it as a foundation for everything else.

1. **Open Facebook and sign in to your personal profile** — On a computer go to facebook.com and sign in; or open the Facebook app on your phone. Use your personal profile: the Page will be created linked to this profile, which will become its administrator.
2. **Open the Page creation flow** — On a computer go to facebook.com/pages/create. On your phone: tap your menu (the three lines or your photo), search for «Pages», then «Create» or «+».
3. **Enter a name and category** — Type your business name (e.g. «Hotel Sole»), choose a category that describes it (e.g. «Hotel», «Restaurant»), and add a brief description. Then press «Create Page».
4. **Confirm you are the administrator** — Since you created it, you are automatically the administrator with full control. Note down the exact name of the Page: you will need it to identify it during the connection.

## Step 1 — Switch your Instagram to a professional account <a id="instagram-professionale"></a>

A «personal» Instagram profile cannot receive messages and comments through an external tool. You need to convert it to «professional» (Business or Creator). It's free, done from the Instagram app on your phone, and you can always switch back.

1. **Open the Instagram app and go to your profile** — Tap your profile photo at the bottom right. Make sure you're on the right account: if you manage more than one, tap the username at the top to switch.
2. **Open Settings** — Tap the three horizontal lines (☰) at the top right, then «Settings and privacy» (or «Settings and activity»).
3. **Find «Account type and tools»** — Scroll to the bottom or use the search bar at the top of settings and type «professional account». Tap «Account type and tools».
4. **Tap «Switch to professional account»** — Follow the guided screens: tap «Continue» whenever prompted.
5. **Choose Business or Creator** — For a hotel, restaurant, B&B, or shop choose «Business». «Creator» is designed for public figures and influencers. If in doubt, choose «Business». Then confirm a category.
6. **Complete and verify** — Skip or complete the optional steps (phone, email, address). When you return to your profile, you should see the chosen category beneath your name: that confirms the account is now professional.

> [!NOTE]
> **If it asks you to link a Page during the conversion**
> During the conversion, Instagram may offer to link a Facebook Page: if your Page appears, link it right away — you will have already completed Step 2 as well. If it doesn't appear, or you skip this point, no problem: we will do it manually below.

## Step 2 — Link Instagram to your Facebook Page <a id="collega-pagina"></a>

Now you associate the two accounts. The most reliable way is to use Meta Business Suite, the free tool that lets you manage your Page and Instagram in one place. You can use it from a computer (website) or from the «Meta Business Suite» app on your phone.

1. **Open Meta Business Suite** — On a computer go to business.facebook.com and sign in with the Facebook profile that administers the Page. On your phone, install and open the «Meta Business Suite» app. It is different from the regular Facebook app.
2. **Select the right Page** — At the top left you will see the name of the Page you are working with. If you have more than one, tap it and choose the correct one.
3. **Open Settings** — Look for the gear icon «Settings» (usually at the bottom left on the website, or in the app menu).
4. **Go to «Instagram accounts»** — In Settings look for the entry «Instagram accounts» (or «Instagram»). Press «Connect account» or «Add».
5. **Sign in with Instagram and authorize** — Enter the username and password of your professional Instagram account and confirm the connection to the Page.
6. **Keep message access enabled in Inbox** — During or immediately after the connection, Meta shows a toggle such as «Allow access to Instagram messages in Inbox»: leave it turned on (ON).
7. **Verify the connection** — On the same «Instagram accounts» screen you should now see your Instagram profile listed as connected. If you see it, Step 2 is complete.

## Step 3 — Enable «Allow access to messages» on Instagram <a id="accesso-messaggi"></a>

> [!WARNING]
> **This is the most commonly forgotten step**
> Even with everything connected correctly, if this toggle is off the agent will receive NO Instagram direct messages. This is the number one cause of «it's not working». Do not skip it.

1. **Open the Instagram app and go to your profile** — Make sure you are on the correct professional account (check the username at the top).
2. **Open Settings and privacy** — Tap the three lines (☰) at the top right, then «Settings and privacy».
3. **Open «Messages and story replies»** — Scroll to the messages section and tap «Messages and story replies». If you can't find it, use the search bar at the top of settings and type «messages».
4. **Open «Message controls»** — Inside this section tap «Message controls».
5. **Find «Connected tools»** — Scroll down to «Connected tools».
6. **Enable «Allow access to messages»** — Turn the «Allow access to messages» toggle ON. If it asks for confirmation, accept. When it's green/active, you're done.

> [!NOTE]
> **Can't see «Connected tools»?**
> This entry only appears on professional accounts linked to a Page. If you can't see it, it means Step 1 (professional account) or Step 2 (linking to the Page) is not complete: go back, complete them, then try again. Also update the Instagram app to the latest version.

## Step 4 — Check that you are an administrator of the Page <a id="ruolo-pagina"></a>

The final connection to Yourang must be done from the Facebook profile that has «Full control» (administrator) of the Page. If you sign in with a profile that only has partial permissions, some authorizations may fail without a clear error message. Here is how to check.

1. **Open Meta Business Suite → Settings** — As in Step 2, enter Meta Business Suite and open the Page Settings.
2. **Go to «People» or «Page access»** — Look for the entry that lists the people with access to the Page (called «People» or «Page access»).
3. **Check your access level** — Next to your name it must say «Full control» or «Administrator». If you only see «Partial access», ask whoever has full control to promote you, or carry out the connection from their profile.

> [!TIP]
> **All set? Go to the connection**
> When all four steps are complete (Page administered, professional Instagram, link active, message access ON), move on to the «Connect Instagram and Facebook» page: from there almost everything is automatic.

# The shop in Yourang

What it's for, when it makes sense to use it, and how it ties into the voice assistant.

_The Shop module lets you register the products and services you offer, organise them in a catalogue, and enable the assistant to sell them or collect orders during calls and chats._

## When to use the shop <a id="quando-serve"></a>

- **Selling complementary products** — A hotel selling spa packages, a restaurant with bottles of wine, a B&B with local specialities. The shop manages price list, stock, and orders.
- **Fixed-price services** — Beauty-centre treatments, transfers, bookable activities. The shop defines the services and their variants.
- **Take-away orders** — For restaurants with phone or WhatsApp orders, the shop is the price list the assistant consults to record dishes correctly.

## Catalogue, orders, inventory <a id="differenza-catalogo-ordini"></a>

- **Catalogue** — The list of everything you sell: products, services, variants, prices, descriptions, images.
- **Orders** — The requests collected through the channels (call, chat, website): status, amount, payment, fulfilment.
- **Inventory (optional)** — For physical products with limited stock, the system tracks availability and warns when an item runs out.

> [!TIP]
> **Even if you don't sell directly**
> Even if you don't manage sales, populating the catalogue with services is useful: the assistant consults it to answer questions about prices, availability, and variants. A living price table is better than a static PDF.

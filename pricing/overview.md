# How pricing works

The two components of Yourang cost: monthly subscription and credit-based consumption.

_Yourang pricing is designed to be predictable and suitable for both small properties and chains. It consists of two elements that combine simply: the subscription and consumption._

## The two cost components <a id="le-due-componenti-del-costo"></a>

- **Monthly subscription** — A fixed fee that grants access to the platform, the plan's features, and a certain amount of included minutes or credits. Billed monthly or annually.
- **Credit-based consumption (wallet)** — Once the included minutes are used up, each additional call minute is deducted from the wallet. The wallet is a prepaid balance you can top up manually or have automatically refilled.

This structure lets you start with a plan aligned to your expected volume and scale transparently as traffic grows, without having to change plans every month.

> [!TIP]
> **Everything is visible from the dashboard**
> At any time you can see how many minutes you have used, how much credit remains in the wallet, and when the next charge will occur. No end-of-month surprises.

## What affects the cost of a call <a id="cosa-influenza-il-costo"></a>

- **Call duration** — Consumption is calculated per minute actually spoken. Short calls cost less; complex calls can last longer.
- **Call type** — Incoming calls, outgoing calls, and transfers to a human operator have separate rates. You'll find the details in the Consumption section of the panel.
- **Chosen AI model** — More advanced models (more natural, better at handling complex conversations) have a higher per-minute cost. You can choose the right model for your use case from the agent configuration.
- **Optional features** — Features such as automatic summary, criteria analysis, and advanced integrations may be included in the plan or available as add-ons.

## Invoicing and VAT <a id="fatturazione-e-iva"></a>

Yourang issues an electronic invoice for every payment, both for subscription renewal and for wallet top-ups. Invoices are available in the Payments section of the dashboard and are emailed to the organization's administrative contact. VAT is calculated based on the registered legal data: for European customers with a valid VAT number, the reverse charge regime applies.
